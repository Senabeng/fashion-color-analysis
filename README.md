## Motivation

This project aims to examine whether fast fashion brands offer a wider range of colors compared to luxury brands. To do this, the color offerings of selected brands were analyzed using a systematic and relatively unbiased data collection process.

## Method

- Collected product images (bags and shoes) for each brand  
- Extracted dominant colors using Python and KMeans clustering  
- Built brand-level color palettes  
- Categorized colors and created comparative distributions  

## Findings

- Loro Piana appears to use extremely light tones, which are often detected as white in the Python-based color extraction process  
- Hermès shows a more neutral and balanced palette  
- H&M and Zara also rely on neutral tones, but introduce a wider variety of colors  

## Insight

While neutral tones dominate across all brands, fast fashion brands provide more color variation.  
This broader range of options, combined with lower price points, may increase customer engagement by offering more accessible and diverse choices.

## Note

This analysis reflects brand-level visual strategies rather than direct consumer purchasing behavior. Results may be influenced by image background and composition.
